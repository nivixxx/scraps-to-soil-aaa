Scraps to Soil

Transforming kitchen waste into organic fertiliser for a sustainable community.

About the Project

Our community project focuses on converting kitchen waste into organic fertiliser. Many households produce wet waste every day which often ends up in landfills. By composting this waste, it can be transformed into nutrient-rich soil that supports plant growth.

Goal & Community Need

Goal Statement

To transform kitchen and organic waste into natural fertiliser to support healthy plant growth, reduce pollution and promote sustainable living in our community.

Need of Community

We realised that houses have organic waste thrown away almost daily, and all this trash goes to landfills and ends up polluting the environment, so we decided to create fertiliser using our community's wet waste, and showcase that their waste could be turned into something valuable.

Planning

Before starting our project we created a plan that included a timeline, success criteria, and the resources needed to complete the composting process.

Gantt Chart

Our project followed a structured timeline including investigation, planning, composting, packaging, and distribution of fertiliser.

Success Criteria

Eco-friendly packaging, clear instructions, equal compost portions, low cost materials and effective community participation.

Action Taken

Part 1: Gathering Wet Waste from our Local Communities

We collected vegetable scraps and kitchen waste from households in our community.

Part 2: Fertiliser Gets Made out of Wet Waste

The organic waste decomposed in compost containers and transformed into nutrient-rich fertiliser.

Part 3: Packaging Fertiliser in Paper Cups

Once the compost was ready, it was placed into biodegradable paper cups with our project logo and wrapped securely.

Part 4: Giving Fertiliser Cups to the Community

The fertiliser cups were given back to the community members who contributed their kitchen waste.

Challenges Faced

Compost Smell

Initially the compost produced a strong smell. With time and patience the decomposition stabilised and produced usable fertiliser.

Deadlines

Managing deadlines within the group was challenging, but communication and reminders helped us stay on track.

Learning

Through this project we developed collaboration, communication, self-management and environmental awareness.

Project Gallery

Reflection

This project helped us understand the importance of sustainable waste management and how community participation can contribute to environmental protection.
